Code Olympia

  • Description

    Round 1: Beginner Problems

    Objective: Assess basic coding skills.

    Languages: Participants can choose two out of the following three programming languages:

    1. Python
    2. Java
    3. C

    Problem Set:

    • Participants will be given a set of simple problems related to loops, conditionals, and basic data structures.
    • Solutions should be implemented in the chosen languages.
    • Problems may include tasks like calculating factorials, finding prime numbers, or reversing strings.

    Scoring: Each correct solution earns points. The faster the solution, the higher the score.

    Note: The first 5 teams will be selected to the next round.

    Round 2: Intermediate Problems

    Objective: Evaluate problem-solving abilities and algorithmic thinking.

    Languages: Participants must code in all three of the following languages:

    1. Python
    2. Java
    3. C

    Problem Set:

    • Participants will face intermediate-level challenges related to data structures, algorithms, and optimization.
    • Problems may involve sorting, searching, dynamic programming, or graph algorithms.
    • Solutions should be efficient and well-optimized.

    Scoring: Points awarded based on correctness, efficiency, and elegance of solutions.

  • Rules

    General Rules

    1. Eligibility:

    • Open to all students and coding enthusiasts.
    • Participants must register in advance.

    2. Fair Play:

    • No plagiarism or copying of code.
    • Participants must solve problems independently.

    3. Time Limits:

    • Each round has a specified time limit.
    • Late submissions receive reduced points.

    4. Code of Conduct:

    • Respect fellow participants and organizers.
    • Follow ethical coding practices.

    5. Prizes:

    • Overall winner receives the Code Olympia Champion title.

  • Description

    Crime Vortex is an engaging crime scene investigation event. This event encompasses a multidisciplinary approach to crime scene investigation, involving various specialized tasks. Participants conduct a systematic search of the crime scene, meticulously observing and documenting all relevant details. This includes capturing photographs and creating sketches to accurately record the scene's layout and evidence. Furthermore, participants focus on identifying, processing, and collecting physical evidence, ensuring proper handling and preservation.

  • Rules

    General Rules

    1. Team Composition: Each team must have 2 to 4 members from different roles.
    2. Crime Scene Protocol: Follow safety instructions; don't disturb the scene before the investigation starts. Conduct a thorough search and handle evidence carefully.
    3. Documentation: Document observations, take photos of the entire scene and close-ups of evidence, and create sketches.
    4. Evidence Collection: Identify, process, and collect all physical evidence carefully, preserving integrity.
    5. Judging Criteria: Evaluation based on thoroughness, accuracy of documentation, professionalism, with extra points for innovative approaches and teamwork.
    6. Code of Conduct: Maintain professionalism and respect; report disputes to organizers; any violations may lead to disqualification.
    7. Disqualification: Violations or failure to follow instructions may result in disqualification.
    8. Event Organizer Authority: Organizers interpret and enforce rules, make decisions, and address issues during the event.

Simuspark Quest

  • Description

    Round 1.1: Online Quiz

    Participants will engage in an online quiz competition covering a spectrum of EEE topics, from fundamental theories to cutting-edge technologies.

    Round 1.2: Scientist Identification

    Teams will be presented with torn images of iconic EEE scientists. Your task is to skilfully reconstruct these images by identifying and piecing together the puzzle elements.

    Each scientist's puzzle will carry clues related to their remarkable contributions, adding an informative and intriguing dimension to the challenge.

    Selection: Teams demonstrating a knack for identifying and assembling the scientist puzzles will move on to the final round.

    Round 2: MATLAB Simulink Competition

    Teams will face a series of simulation challenges, ranging from circuit design and control systems to dynamic system modelling. The competition will demand a blend of theoretical understanding and hands-on MATLAB Simulink proficiency.

    Accuracy of simulations, efficiency in problem-solving, and creativity in optimizing system performance will be evaluated.

    Grand Finale: The team demonstrating the most comprehensive understanding and application of MATLAB Simulink will be crowned the "Simuspark Quest Champions" of GDSCfest24.

  • Rules

    General Rules

    • Minimum 2 to maximum 4 participants will be permitted as a team. No any external devices required from the participants.
    • In the Quiz, points will be awarded for each correct answer. The total points earned will determine team rankings.
    • The top-performing teams will advance to the next round based on their cumulative points.
    • Points will be awarded for originality, effectiveness of simulations, and feasibility of proposed solutions. The total points earned will determine the grand champion.
    • Judges' decisions are final and cannot be contested.
    • Participants should maintain a positive and collaborative spirit throughout the event.
